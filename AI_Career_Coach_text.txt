 ABSTRACT 
 
The rapid evolution of the job market has increased the complexity of 
career planning and skill development for job seekers. Many candidates 
struggle to identify skill gaps, optimize their resumes for Applicant 
Tracking Systems (ATS), and locate job opportunities that align with their 
qualifications and experience. To address these challenges, this project 
presents AI Career Coach, an intelligent career guidance platform 
powered by artificial intelligence and natural language processing 
techniques. 
The proposed system analyzes user-uploaded resumes to extract 
essential information such as skills, education, work experience, and 
achievements. Using advanced matching algorithms, the extracted data 
is compared with real job descriptions to generate ranked job 
recommendations based on similarity scores. The platform also performs 
skill gap analysis by identifying missing or outdated competencies relative 
to target roles, providing users with clear insights into areas requiring 
improvement. 
In addition, a smart resume optimization module enhances resume 
content by improving structure, wording, and keyword usage to increase 
compatibility with ATS systems. The system further recommends 
personalized learning paths, including online courses, certifications, and 
practical projects, to help users bridge identified skill gaps. By integrating 
multiple intelligent modules into a unified platform, AI Career Coach offers 
a comprehensive solution for career development, supporting informed 
decision-making and continuous professional growth. 
the platform is designed with a modular and scalable architecture that 
allows seamless integration of additional AI models and data sources in 
the future. The system emphasizes usability and accessibility by providing 
a user-friendly interface that delivers clear visualizations and actionable 
insights.  
From a technical perspective, the project demonstrates the practical 
application of NLP and AI-driven recommendation systems in real-world 
career guidance scenarios. Overall, AI Career Coach contributes to 
enhancing employability by transforming static resumes into dynamic 
career development tools.

1 INTRODUCTION  
  
In today’s rapidly evolving job market, securing employment has become 
increasingly challenging, particularly for fresh graduates and early-career 
professionals. Modern hiring processes rely heavily on automated 
screening technologies such as Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS), which 
evaluate résumés based on structure, keywords, formatting, and overall 
relevance before they are reviewed by human recruiters. As a result, many 
qualified candidates are eliminated at early stages of the recruitment 
pipeline not due to a lack of competence, but because their résumés are 
not optimized to meet ATS requirements.  
The core challenge facing job seekers is not a shortage of talent or 
ambition, but rather the absence of clear and practical guidance on how 
to present their skills in alignment with job requirements. Applicants often 
submit large numbers of applications without receiving feedback or 
understanding why they are not progressing in the recruitment process. 
Without visibility into résumé weaknesses, missing skills, or keyword 
mismatches, job seekers are left to guess how to improve their chances 
of success, leading to inefficiency, frustration, and repeated rejection.  
The AI Career Coach project is designed to address these challenges by 
delivering an intelligent, fully automated system that supports users 
throughout the career development and job application process. By 
leveraging advanced artificial intelligence techniques—particularly 
Natural Language Processing (NLP)—the system analyzes résumés, 
interprets job descriptions, and generates personalized, actionable 
insights. These insights help candidates clearly understand their 
strengths, weaknesses, and areas requiring improvement.  
The project aims to build a comprehensive, end-to-end pipeline capable 
of processing résumés in PDF and DOCX formats with accurate text 
extraction and structural analysis. It extracts essential information such as 
skills, professional experience, education, achievements, and relevant 
keywords using modern NLP models. The system then compares 
résumés with job descriptions to generate an objective Match Score that 
reflects how well a candidate aligns with a specific role. In addition, it 
performs detailed skills gap analysis to identify missing or

1.1 Problem Definition  
In the modern job market, job seekers—particularly fresh graduates and 
early-career professionals—face a significant challenge due to the lack of 
visibility and feedback in the application process. Employer adoption of 
automated Applicant Tracking Systems 
(ATS) has transformed 
recruitment, but these systems often filter out qualified candidates before 
a human recruiter ever reviews their application. ATS software scans 
résumés for specific keywords, formatting, and structured data, meaning 
that even skilled applicants may be excluded simply because their résumé 
lacked the exact terms or design required by the system. 
This leads to many résumés being discarded without transparent or useful 
feedback, making the job search feel like a “black hole” where applications 
disappear with no explanation, leaving candidates uncertain about how to 
improve future submissions.   
Many candidates are unaware of how to optimize their résumés for ATS 
compatibility, including how to structure content and select appropriate 
industry-specific 
keywords 
that 
align 
with 
automated 
parsing 
requirements. Without this guidance, applicants may inadvertently 
diminish their chances of passing initial screenings, despite having the 
necessary qualifications. 
In addition to struggling with résumé optimization, job seekers often find 
the application process inefficient because they receive little to no 
feedback on why their applications were unsuccessful.

1.5 Related Work  
The development of intelligent career guidance systems has attracted 
significant attention in both academic research and industry practice, 
particularly with the increasing use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Natural 
Language Processing (NLP) to enhance employability and recruitment 
outcomes. A wide range of studies and commercial platforms have 
explored automated résumé analysis, skill extraction, and personalized 
career recommendations as mechanisms to support job seekers in 
navigating modern hiring processes.  
One prominent category of existing solutions focuses on optimizing 
résumés for Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS). Commercial tools such as 
JobScan and Resumatch are designed to improve keyword alignment and 
résumé formatting to increase compatibility with automated screening 
systems. These tools typically provide feedback related to résumé 
structure, keyword density, and matching against job descriptions. 
However, they often offer limited insight into deeper skill deficiencies and 
generally 
do 
not 
include 
personalized 
or 
structured 
learning 
recommendations to address identified gaps.  
Another group of solutions consists of AI-powered career guidance 
platforms such as VMock and CareerLift. These platforms utilize AI and 
NLP models to analyze résumés, assess professional skills and 
experience, and suggest areas for improvement. In some cases, they 
provide benchmarking against industry standards or peer profiles. Despite 
these capabilities, their recommendation mechanisms are often 
constrained, focusing primarily on résumé enhancement rather than  

--- PAGE 16 ---

offering actionable and targeted learning paths that support long-term skill 
development and career progression.  
Academic research has further highlighted the importance of skills gap 
analysis in improving employment outcomes. Numerous studies 
demonstrate that AI-driven systems can compare job descriptions with  
candidate profiles to identify missing or underdeveloped skills and 
propose targeted learning interventions.  
These approaches frequently rely on techniques such as embedding-
based semantic similarity, named entity recognition for skill identification, 
and machine learning models for classification and matching. While these 
methods have shown promising results, many research solutions remain 
theoretical or lack integration into fully functional, user-oriented systems.  
Despite the progress achieved by existing platforms and research efforts, 
several limitations remain evident. Many current solutions concentrate 
either on résumé formatting or on skill assessment, with limited integration 
between the two. Personalized learning recommendations are often 
generic and insufficiently tailored to individual career goals. Furthermore, 
few systems provide a comprehensive end-to-end pipeline that spans 
résumé ingestion, skill extraction, job matching, and actionable career 
guidance. Robust support for multi-format résumé processing, particularly 
PDF and DOCX files, is also limited across many existing tools.  
The AI Career Coach project aims to build upon these prior approaches 
by introducing a comprehensive, end-to-end system that integrates 
résumé analysis, Match Score computation, skills gap identification, and 
personalized learning recommendations within a unified framework. By 
combining accurate multi-format text extraction, NLP-based skill analysis, 
and actionable career guidance, the project addresses the limitations of 
existing tools and research, delivering a more complete, practical, and 
user-centered solution for career development and employability 
enhancement.

2.2.1.8  Risk Assessment and Mitigation  
Risk Assessment:  
AI Career Coach — Intelligent Career Guidance Platform 
Based on the defined system requirements, architecture, and AI-driven 
workflows of the AI Career Coach platform, several potential risks have been 
identified. These risks may affect system performance, accuracy, reliability, 
and overall project success if not properly managed. 
1. Third-Party API Dependency  
The platform relies on multiple third-party services, including AI/NLP libraries, 
OCR tools, and job data sources. Any changes in API availability, pricing 
models, usage limits, or service discontinuation could negatively impact core 
functionalities such as CV analysis, ATS scoring, and job matching.  
Additionally, dependency on external job platforms (e.g., LinkedIn redirection) 
introduces a risk of broken workflows if URL structures or access policies 
change.  
2. Data Quality & Resume Variability Risk  
The accuracy of CV analysis and ATS scoring heavily depends on the quality 
and structure of uploaded resumes. Variations in resume formats, language 
styles, scanned documents, or poorly formatted files may lead to incomplete 
text extraction or incorrect entity recognition.  
This risk can result in inaccurate skill extraction, leading to incomplete or 
misleading candidate profiles.  
It may also cause incorrect ATS scores, which can affect the evaluation and 
ranking of applicants within     the system.  
Furthermore, the risk can result in weak job matching and recommendations, 
reducing the overall effectiveness and reliability of the AI Career Coach 
platform.  
  
  

--- PAGE 36 ---
 
Chapter Two: System Analysis and Design   
36 
 
3. AI Model Accuracy & Bias Risk  
The AI Career Coach system relies on large language models accessed 
through external APIs to perform key tasks such as resume analysis, skill 
extraction, job matching, and optimization. Although pre-trained models 
provide powerful language understanding, they may reflect biases present in 
their training data and favor certain writing styles or terminology. Because 
these models were not specifically trained on every possible job domain or 
resume format, their outputs may occasionally underperform on real-world 
resumes with diverse structures. Such accuracy and bias risks could affect 
user trust and the perceived fairness of recommendations if not carefully 
monitored and evaluated. 
4. Performance  
The AI Career Coach platform performs a range of computationally intensive 
operations including document parsing, NLP pipelines, semantic embedding 
generation, and inference requests to external language model APIs. These 
processes require significant processing resources and may impact system 
responsiveness under high load. As the number of users and uploaded 
resumes increases, the system may encounter latency spikes or fail to meet 
real-time performance expectations. This risk is particularly apparent when 
handling large files, simultaneous API calls, or complex semantic matching 
requests, potentially affecting the overall user experience if not properly 
monitored and scaled. 
5. Data Privacy & Security Risks  
The system processes sensitive personal data, including resume content, 
employment history, and skills and qualifications. Protecting this information is 
critical to maintaining user trust and complying with privacy regulations.  
Any failure in implementing proper security controls—such as data encryption, 
secure authentication, or access control mechanisms—could result in data 
leaks or unauthorized access. Such breaches may lead to serious privacy 
concerns and legal consequences for the platform.  
6. System Reliability & Availability  
 
 
 

--- PAGE 37 ---
 
Chapter Two: System Analysis and Design   
37 
 
Maintaining consistent system availability is critical for user experience. Risks 
related to cloud service downtime, server failures, or inadequate backup 
mechanisms could cause service interruptions, data loss, or degraded system 
performance.  
 
 
These issues are particularly impactful during peak usage periods, when many 
users rely on the platform simultaneously. Proper monitoring and redundancy 
measures are essential to minimize such risks and ensure smooth operation.

The AI Career Coach platform faces a combination of technical, operational,

2.3.12.3  Model Selection Justification and Comparison  
In the AI Career Coach project, multiple implementation approaches were 
evaluated for   
performing résumé analysis, skill extraction, and job matching. These 
approaches include building custom Machine Learning or Deep Learning 
models from scratch, as well as leveraging pre-trained models and APIs 
provided by established AI platforms such as Hugging Face and external 
AI services. After careful evaluation, the project adopted a hybrid 

--- PAGE 64 ---
 
Chapter Two: System Analysis and Design

approach based on pre-trained models and APIs rather than developing 
standalone ML/DL models from the ground up.  
Use of APIs and Pre-trained Models  
APIs and pre-trained models offer a practical and efficient solution for 
complex Natural Language Processing (NLP) tasks. Platforms such as 
Hugging Face provide state-of-theart transformer-based models that have 
been trained on massive datasets, enabling high accuracy in tasks like 
semantic similarity, entity extraction, and text classification. By using these 
models, the system benefits from advanced language understanding 
without the need for extensive training resources.  
Limitations of Building Custom ML/DL Models  
Developing Machine Learning or Deep Learning models from scratch 
requires large labeled datasets, significant computational resources, and 
extended training and tuning time. In the context of a graduation project 
with limited time and resources, collecting and annotating high-quality 
résumé and job description datasets is both challenging and 
timeconsuming. Additionally, training deep neural networks introduces 
risks related to overfitting, model instability, and performance 
inconsistency.  
Performance and Accuracy Considerations  
Pre-trained transformer models consistently outperform traditional 
machine learning techniques in NLP tasks due to their contextual 
understanding of language. These   
models capture semantic relationships between skills, job requirements, 
and experience descriptions more effectively than classical approaches 
such as TF-IDF or rule-based systems. APIs further optimize inference 
speed and scalability by handling deployment and optimization internally.  
Scalability and Maintainability  
Using APIs and pre-trained models significantly improves system 
scalability and maintainability. Model updates, improvements, and 
optimizations are handled by the

research without requiring major architectural changes. In contrast, 
maintaining custom ML/DL models would require continuous retraining

Development Efficiency  
From a software engineering perspective, integrating APIs and Hugging 
Face models accelerates development and allows the project to focus on 
system design, user experience, and intelligent feature integration rather 
than low-level model training. This  approach aligns with real-world 
industry practices, where AI-powered systems often rely on reliable third-
party models and services.  
Final Decision  
Based on accuracy, resource efficiency, scalability, and development 
constraints, the AI Career Coach project adopts pre-trained NLP models 
and AI APIs as the core intelligence layer. This decision ensures robust 
performance, faster development, and alignment with industry-standard AI 
deployment practices, while still allowing future enhancement or finetuning 
if required.

3.2  Backend Implementation 
The backend of the AI Study Assistant system was implemented using 
the FastAPI framework due to its high performance, asynchronous 
capabilities, and ease of integration with modern AI technologies. The 
backend acts as the core component responsible for handling document 
processing, embedding generation, vector database communication, 
retrieval operations, and interaction with Large Language Models (LLMs). 
 
The system follows a modular architecture to improve maintainability, 
scalability, and code organization. Each module is designed to perform a 
specific task independently, which simplifies future development and 
debugging processes. 
 
The backend workflow begins when the user uploads a document through 
the application interface. The uploaded file is received by the API and 
passed to the document processing module, where the text is extracted, 
cleaned, and divided into smaller chunks. These chunks are then 
converted into vector embeddings using the embedding model and stored 
inside the vector database for semantic retrieval. 
 
When the user sends a question, the backend converts the query into an 
embedding vector and searches for the most relevant document chunks 
inside the vector database. The retrieved context is then combined with 
the user query and forwarded to the Large Language Model to generate 
a context-aware response. Finally, the generated answer is returned to 
the user through the API response. 
 
The backend was developed using asynchronous programming 
techniques to improve response speed and allow efficient handling of 
multiple requests simultaneously. FastAPI provides native support for 
asynchronous 
endpoints, 
which 
enhances 
the 
overall 
system 
performance, especially during AI model communication and database 
operations. 
 
The project structure was divided into multiple components to separate 
responsibilities clearly. The main modules of the backend include: 
 
 
➢ API Routes Module : Responsible for handling HTTP requests and 
routing operations.

3.2.5 AI Query Processing Workflow 
The AI Query Processing Workflow represents the complete operational 
pipeline followed by the system to process user requests and generate 
intelligent responses. The workflow combines document processing, 
semantic representation, vector retrieval, and Large Language Model 
generation to provide context-aware answers and educational 
assistance. 
The workflow begins when the user uploads one or more documents 
through the application interface. The uploaded files are first validated 
and processed by the document processing module, where textual 
content is extracted and cleaned before being divided into smaller 
semantic chunks. 
After preprocessing, the generated chunks are converted into embedding 
vectors using the Cohere embedding model. These vectors are then 
stored inside the Qdrant vector database together with their 
corresponding metadata and document references. 
When the user submits a query through the chat interface, the system 
converts the query into an embedding vector using the same embedding 
model to ensure semantic consistency between stored documents and 
user input. 
The generated query embedding is then compared with stored vectors 
inside the vector database using semantic similarity search operations. 
The system retrieves the most contextually relevant document chunks 
based on vector similarity calculations. 
After retrieval, the selected contextual information is combined with the 
original user query and forwarded to the Large Language Model through 
the Groq API. The LLM analyzes both the query and the retrieved context 
to generate an intelligent and context-aware response. 
The generated answer is finally returned to the frontend interface and 
displayed to the user in real time.
